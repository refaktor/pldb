Automatically Programmed Tool, aka Automatically Programmed Tool, is a programming language created in 1956.

#752on PLDB 67Years Old

APT or Automatically Programmed Tool is a high-level computer programming language most commonly used to generate instructions for numerically controlled machine tools. Douglas T. Ross is considered by many to be the father of APT: as head of the newly created Computer Applications Group of the Servomechanisms Laboratory at MIT in 1956, he led its technical effort.
